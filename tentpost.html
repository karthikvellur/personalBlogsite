About the Author

Pop I. Sailorman is currently Professor Emeritus at Salty Dog's School of Nautical Nonsense. After graduating Summa Cum Getsome at the prestigious School of Hard Knocks, Professor Sailorman spends his time pondering philosophy and encouraging healthy eating habits in today’s youth.

You may know him from his popular Ted Talks, “I am what I am, and that's all that I am”, and “If I'm not me, who am I? And If I'm somebody else, why do I look like me?”. He is currently the Editor-In-Chief of this blog; if you have questions about this content feel free to contact us via the link provided on this website.

Our Favorite Tents

“Camping has become one of my most beloved pastimes. I take a fierce delight in swinging a pak o my back or into a canoe and heading for the hills or lakes. In my opinion, camping can be the greatest expression of free will, personal independence, innate ability, and resourcefulness possible today in our industrialized, urbanized existence. Regardless of how miserable or how splendid the circumstances, the sheer experience of camping seems a total justification for doing it.” ― Anne LaBastille
